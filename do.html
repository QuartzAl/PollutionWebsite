What can we do?

To help solve this prolonged problem of pollution we can all contribute to mitigate and/or respond towards the effects of pollution

Responding to the effects:


  • Volunteer for cleanup events: Organize or participate in community cleanups to remove trash and debris from parks, beaches, or neighborhoods.
  • Report illegal dumping: If you see someone dumping waste illegally, report it to the local authorities. Early intervention can prevent environmental damage.
  • Support land restoration projects: Look for organizations working to restore degraded land due to pollution. You can volunteer your time or donate resources.
  • Advocate for stricter regulations: Contact your local representatives and voice your support for stricter laws and regulations to prevent land pollution.


There are also efforts to migitate the effects of pollution



  • Compost food scraps and yard waste: This reduces the amount of organic waste going to landfills and creates nutrient-rich fertilizer for your garden, improving soil health.
  • Minimize use of herbicides and pesticides: Opt for natural pest control methods or targeted solutions to reduce chemical contamination in the soil.
  • Choose sustainable products: Look for products made from recycled materials or with minimal packaging to reduce waste generation and resource extraction that can lead to land degradation.

Responding to the effects:


  • Monitor air quality reports: Check air quality forecasts in your area and limit strenuous outdoor activity when pollution levels are high.
  • Report excessive smoke or burning: If you see excessive smoke or illegal burning, report it to the appropriate authorities. A this is a prominent problem in Surabaya or even Indonesia with irresponsible burning of trash creating extra potential for green house gases
  • Use air purifiers: Consider using air purifiers in your home, especially if you live in a polluted area or have respiratory problems.
  • Stay informed about health risks: Educate yourself about the health risks associated with air pollution and take precautions to protect yourself, especially children and the elderly.


There are also efforts to migitate the effects of pollution



  • Reduce car usage: Walk, bike, carpool, or use public transportation whenever possible. This reduces traffic congestion and vehicle emissions.
  • Conserve energy at home: Turn off lights and electronics when not in use. Opt for energy-efficient appliances and adjust thermostats for moderate temperatures.
  • Choose eco-friendly cleaning products: Look for low-VOC (volatile organic compounds) cleaning products to minimize indoor air pollution. This is also a fact rarely known by people in Surabaya

Responding to the effects:


  • Conserve water: Simple practices like taking shorter showers, fixing leaky faucets, and watering lawns efficiently can help conserve precious water resources.
  • Report water pollution incidents: If you observe any suspicious discharge or pollution in waterways, report it to the environmental authorities.
  • Educate others about water conservation: Talk to friends and family about the importance of conserving water and reducing water pollution.
  • Do not waste drinking water: Drinking water is a very important resource and is limited as well. Being able to not waste drinking water appropriately will help

  • Dispose of chemicals properly: Never pour used oil, paint, or harsh chemicals down the drain. Look for designated drop-off locations for safe disposal.
  • Minimize medication waste: Don't flush unused or expired medications down the toilet. Look for take-back programs or responsible disposal options at pharmacies or hospitals.
  • Clean up after pets: Always pick up after your pet to prevent waste from contaminating water sources with harmful bacteria.
